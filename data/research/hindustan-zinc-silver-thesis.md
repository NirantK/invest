# Hindustan Zinc Silver Operations Overview

Hindustan Zinc Limited (HZL) is a significant player in the global silver market, ranked as the world's 3rd largest silver producer. It is also uniquely positioned as India's only integrated producer of zinc, lead, and silver. Silver is not mined directly but is a high-value byproduct recovered during the company's primary zinc-lead mining and smelting operations. HZL has invested in dedicated infrastructure to maximize this recovery, including a silver refinery in Pantnagar with a capacity of 500 MT, commissioned in 2012, and ongoing projects to improve silver recovery from lead concentrate.

The contribution of silver to the company's total revenue has been substantial and growing. In Fiscal Year 2023, silver revenue was ₹4,382 crore, accounting for approximately 12.8% of the total consolidated revenue of ₹34,252 crore. In FY24, silver revenue was approximately ₹4,076 crore, representing about 14.1% of the total revenue of ₹28,932 crore. This share grew significantly in FY25, with silver revenue reaching ₹6,130 crore, or about 18.0% of the total revenue of ₹34,083 crore. Revenue breakdowns for FY20-FY22 were not explicitly provided in the source documents.

In terms of production volume, HZL has shown a general upward trend over the years, with some fluctuations. The historical saleable silver production in metric tonnes is as follows:
- FY20: 610 tonnes
- FY21: 706 tonnes
- FY22: 647 tonnes
- FY23: 714 tonnes
- FY24: 746 tonnes
- FY25: 687 tonnes
- FY26: 627 tonnes (full year), with 176 tonnes produced in Q4 FY26.

# Impact Of Silver Price On Financials

The price of silver has a significant and increasingly material impact on Hindustan Zinc's key financial metrics, including EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization), EBIT (Earnings Before Interest and Taxes), and consequently, EPS (Earnings Per Share). This sensitivity stems from silver being an extremely high-margin byproduct. 

An analysis of the company's FY23 segment disclosure reveals the extent of this profitability: the silver segment generated revenue of ₹4,382 crore and a segment result (a proxy for EBIT) of ₹3,841 crore. This implies an exceptionally high segment EBIT margin of approximately 88%. Because the costs associated with silver are largely absorbed by the primary zinc-lead operations, most of the revenue from silver sales flows directly to the bottom line.

The contribution of precious metals (primarily silver) to the company's total EBIT has been substantial, though variable, highlighting its importance to overall profitability. The contribution percentages were:
- FY21: 41%
- FY22: 28%
- FY23: 27%
- FY24: 45%
- FY25: 38%

The company's management has explicitly acknowledged this positive financial leverage. In the FY24 report, the company highlighted its strategy of "maximisation of silver production to leverage the rising silver prices" as a key factor in its performance. As the revenue contribution from silver has grown, reaching 18% in FY25, the company's consolidated margins and earnings have become more sensitive to fluctuations in silver prices. A rise in silver prices directly boosts revenue and profitability, while a decline can have a notable negative impact. The provided documents do not contain a specific sensitivity analysis table (e.g., the exact impact of a $1/oz price change on EPS), but the high margin and significant EBIT contribution clearly indicate a strong positive correlation between silver prices and HZL's financial performance.

# Company Guidance On Silver

Hindustan Zinc's management has consistently communicated a clear strategy focused on expanding its silver production to capitalize on its value. In various investor presentations and reports, the company has outlined ambitious growth targets and the initiatives to achieve them.

The company has set a clear vision to significantly increase its silver output. The guidance includes:
- **Intermediate-Term Target:** To increase silver production to 1,000 metric tonnes (MT) per annum. This goal is planned to be achieved through specific technological interventions like the implementation of fumer technology and enhanced lead-silver recovery processes. These projects are expected to contribute an additional 50-60 tonnes per annum (TPA).
- **Long-Term Target:** To further expand silver production to 1,500 MT. This ambition is aligned with the company's broader strategic plan to grow its total metal production capacity to 2 million tonnes per annum (Mtpa).

Regarding near-term guidance, for Fiscal Year 2025, the company initially provided a production guidance of 750-775 MT. This was later revised in a February 2025 presentation to 700-710 MT. The actual production for FY25 was 687 MT. Management commentary emphasizes that silver production has already grown over 2 times in the last decade and 15 times in the last two decades, underscoring their long-term commitment to expanding this revenue stream.

# Overview Of Silver Investment In India

Investors in India have several avenues to gain exposure to silver, each with distinct characteristics. The primary methods include direct stock investment, Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs), and mutual funds.

1.  **Indirect Exposure via Stock (Hindustan Zinc - HZL):** Investing in the equity of Hindustan Zinc offers a leveraged but indirect exposure to silver. HZL is India's sole integrated producer of zinc, lead, and silver, with silver being a high-margin by-product of its primary mining operations. While silver's contribution to the company's earnings is significant (contributing ~38% to EBIT in FY25) and has been increasing, the stock's performance is still predominantly driven by zinc prices. This makes HZL a hybrid investment, suitable for those seeking combined exposure to industrial metals (zinc) with a silver 'kicker', potential dividend yield, and upside from the company's silver production expansion projects. It is not a pure-play investment for tracking silver prices directly.

2.  **Direct Exposure via Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs):** For investors seeking pure-play exposure to the domestic price of silver, ETFs are described as the preferable and 'cleanest' method. These funds aim to track the price of physical silver with low tracking error and transparent costs. They are traded on stock exchanges like shares, offering liquidity and ease of access. Examples include the Nippon India Silver ETF (SILVERBEES) and the Zerodha Silver ETF (SILVERCASE). This route is ideal for investors who want to speculate on or hedge with silver prices without the complexities of storing physical metal.

3.  **Exposure via Mutual Funds:** Another option is to invest in mutual fund Fund-of-Funds (FoFs) that, in turn, invest in silver ETFs. This provides an alternative way to access the returns of silver ETFs through a mutual fund structure. However, it's important to note that this method typically involves layered costs, as the FoF will have its own expense ratio on top of the expense ratio of the underlying ETF it holds.

# Recommended Strategy For Silver Exposure

The most effective way to gain silver exposure in India depends on the investor's specific goals, risk tolerance, and desired investment purity. The two primary routes, investing in Hindustan Zinc (HZL) stock versus silver Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs), offer distinct risk-reward profiles.

**1. For Direct, Pure-Play Silver Exposure: Silver ETFs**

For investors whose primary goal is to track the domestic price of silver, silver ETFs are the most suitable and efficient instrument. 

*   **Key Options:** Prominent examples include the Nippon India Silver ETF (SILVERBEES) and the Zerodha Silver ETF (SILVERCASE). Other asset management companies also offer similar products.
*   **Pros:**
    *   **Direct Correlation:** The value of the ETF unit is designed to move in line with the price of physical silver.
    *   **Transparency:** Holdings (physical silver bars) and costs (Total Expense Ratio, or TER) are clearly disclosed. For example, SILVERCASE has a TER of ~0.33%.
    *   **Liquidity:** Traded on stock exchanges like the NSE, offering ease of buying and selling during market hours.
    *   **Simplicity:** Avoids the complexities of company-specific operational risks, management decisions, and the influence of other commodity markets.
*   **Cons:**
    *   No dividends or yield.
    *   Subject to tracking error and expense ratios, which slightly reduce returns compared to the spot price.

**2. For Leveraged, Hybrid Exposure: Hindustan Zinc (HZL) Stock**

Investing in HZL stock provides exposure to silver, but it is bundled with the company's larger zinc business and operational factors.

*   **Pros:**
    *   **Operational Leverage:** As a producer, HZL's profits from silver can increase at a faster rate than the silver price itself, especially given its very high-margin silver segment (~88% EBIT margin).
    *   **Growth Potential:** Investors gain exposure to the company's strategic initiatives to increase silver production towards 1,000-1,500 MT.
    *   **Dividend Yield:** HZL has a history of paying substantial dividends, providing an additional source of return.
    *   **Diversification:** Exposure is linked to both zinc and silver markets.
*   **Cons:**
    *   **Indirect Exposure:** The stock price is primarily driven by zinc prices and overall market sentiment, not just silver. A rally in silver could be offset by a downturn in zinc.
    *   **Company-Specific Risk:** Investors are exposed to operational issues, management execution, regulatory changes, and other business risks unrelated to the silver price.

**Conclusion and Recommendation:**

*   **If your goal is pure silver beta and to directly benefit from a potential rise in silver prices, Silver ETFs are the clear and recommended choice.** They offer clean, transparent, and low-cost exposure.
*   **If you are a more sophisticated investor looking for a hybrid play with potential for alpha from operational improvements and are comfortable with the risks of the zinc market and mining operations, HZL stock is a viable, albeit indirect, way to invest in silver.** It should be viewed as an investment in a leading mining company with a significant and profitable silver byproduct, not as a direct silver proxy.
