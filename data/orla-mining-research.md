## 4. GROWTH PIPELINE

### South Railroad Project (Nevada, USA -- Tier 1 Jurisdiction)
- **Updated Feasibility Study released Jan 15, 2026** -- construction approved
- **After-tax NPV (5%): $1.7 billion**
- **After-tax IRR: 95%** (at $4,500/oz gold assumption per LinkedIn post)
- **Initial capex: $395 million** (up from $190M in 2022 FS, due to inflation and flowsheet upgrades)
- Open-pit heap leach operation on the Carlin Trend
- **Total ore to leach pad: 71.9M tonnes at 0.77 g/t Au**
- **Contained gold: 1,604,000 oz**
- Permitting kicked off Aug 2025 with BLM Notice of Intent
- **Self-funded from existing cash flow** -- this is a key bull case point
- NI 43-101 technical report filed Mar 2, 2026

### Camino Rojo Underground Project (Mexico)
- **Positive PEA released Feb 19, 2026**
- **After-tax NPV (5%): $1.3 billion**
- **After-tax IRR: 30%** (at $3,100/oz gold)
- **Throughput: 8,000 tonnes per day sulphide operation**
- **Annual gold production: ~215,000 oz/yr** (per Mexico Business News)
- This is a *separate* development option on top of the existing oxide open-pit
- The underground resource extends from the base of the oxide pit and includes Camino Rojo Sulphides and Zone 22
- Initial underground mineral resource delivered Jun 2025

### Musselwhite Exploration Upside
- Dec 2025: Orla confirmed **2-kilometre gold trend extension** at Musselwhite
- 2026 planning targets continued mine trend extension and resource expansion
- Minimum 6-year mine life with "significant upside"

## 5. RISKS

### Jurisdictional
- **Mexico**: Camino Rojo is the flagship producing asset. Orla received new permits in Mar 2026 to extend operations, reinforcing long-term commitment. CEO has 14 years direct experience in Mexico (per Mexico Business News interview). However, Mexico's regulatory environment for mining has been tightening
- **Panama**: Company has high-risk, non-operating exposure in Panama (per PESTLE analysis). This is a legacy/dormant situation
- **Nevada/Canada**: South Railroad (Nevada) and Musselwhite (Ontario) are Tier-1 jurisdictions -- this is the direction the company is pivoting toward

### Operational/Financial
- **Capital-intensive reinvestment phase**: South Railroad capex of $395M is significant. The bear case (Seeking Alpha, Apr 2, 2026) warns that "Orla enters a capital-intensive reinvestment phase, amid rising cost pressures and uncertain gold price tailwinds"
- **Rising costs**: Inflation has already pushed South Railroad capex from $190M to $395M
- **Gold price dependency**: The PEA and FS economics assume gold at $3,100-4,500/oz. A sustained gold price decline would materially impact returns
- **Q4 net margin only 7%**: Despite record revenue, the net margin was thin at 7% in Q4 2025, which "tests bullish high growth narratives" (Simply Wall St)
- **Stock volatility**: 16.8% decline in one week, 35.7% YTD gain, 21% pullback noted by Seeking Alpha (as of early Apr 2026)

## 8. BULL vs BEAR SUMMARY

### Bull Case
- **Production doubling**: From ~130k oz (pre-Musselwhite) to 300k oz in 2025, path to 500k oz
- **Self-funded growth**: South Railroad ($1.7B NPV, 95% IRR) being built with internal cash flow
- **Camino Rojo Underground optionality**: $1.3B NPV at PEA stage -- this is a free option on top of existing operations
- **Cheap valuation**: 9x 2026 earnings for a gold producer with this growth profile
- **Tier-1 jurisdiction shift**: Nevada and Ontario de-risk the portfolio vs. Mexico-only
- **Record FCF**: $133M in 2025, strong balance sheet ($420.8M cash)
- **Gold macro backdrop**: Gold at/near all-time highs, structural supply deficit

### Bear Case (per Seeking Alpha "Rally Priced In, Risks Emerging", Apr 2, 2026)
- **Capital-intensive reinvestment phase**: $395M capex for South Railroad is a big outlay
- **Rising costs**: Capex inflation already doubled South Railroad's price tag
- **Thin margins**: Q4 net margin was only 7% despite record gold prices
- **Gold price risk**: If gold falls from current ~$3,000+/oz levels, economics deteriorate fast
- **Mexico risk**: Camino Rojo is still a major producing asset, and Mexico's mining regulatory environment is uncertain
- **Execution risk**: Building a new mine (South Railroad) while operating two others simultaneously
- **Stock already rallied 35.7% YTD** before the recent 21% pullback -- timing risk
