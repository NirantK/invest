# Executive Summary

The collective 2026 investment outlook from Rick Rule, John Hathaway, and Tavi Costa points to a continued, multi-year bull market in natural resources. All three experts agree that a period of significant underinvestment in supply, coupled with rising demand and macroeconomic instability, has created a favorable environment for commodities. Rick Rule emphasizes the structural supply deficits in oil & gas, which he calls the 'best sector' for the next five years, as well as in uranium and copper. John Hathaway projects a materially higher gold price, potentially reaching $4,000 per ounce within two to three years, driven by institutional portfolio reallocations and favorable M&A dynamics within the mining sector. Tavi Costa, now at Azuria Capital, argues that the market is in the early stages of a new commodity supercycle, presenting asymmetric investment opportunities in precious metals, critical metals, and energy, fueled by structural trends like the AI buildout and onshoring.

*   **Gold as Macro Insurance and a Portfolio Staple:** There is a consensus that gold serves as essential portfolio insurance against macroeconomic uncertainty and policy shifts. Hathaway highlights a trend of major financial institutions recommending significant allocations (10-20%) to gold as a replacement for bonds.
*   **Favorable M&A Environment:** John Hathaway specifically points to mergers and acquisitions as a key return driver for mining equities, with a strategy of owning quality assets that larger companies will feel compelled to acquire.

# Rick Rule Analysis

## Investment Thesis

Rick Rule's core investment thesis for 2026 is centered on the consequences of prolonged underinvestment in the natural resources sector. He argues that the industry began its necessary capital expenditure (CAPEX) cycle '15–20 years too late,' which has resulted in a structural scarcity across essential commodities like copper, uranium, and hydrocarbons. This long-term neglect and capital starvation have created a scenario where supply cannot easily meet rising demand, setting the stage for a multi-year opportunity in these sectors.

## Key Arguments

Several key arguments support Rick Rule's thesis. A central point is the delayed CAPEX cycle in mining, which he believes has created a 'perfect setup' for a boom in commodities like uranium and a 'copper crisis' due to 30 years of underinvestment. Another argument is the necessity and attractiveness of 'hated' sectors, particularly oil and gas, which he views as having low risk and high margins due to capital starvation improving balance sheets. He also strongly advocates for gold as 'portfolio insurance' against macro uncertainty and a significant anticipated decline in the US dollar's purchasing power, which he projects could drop by 75% over the next decade. Finally, he emphasizes that incumbent producers are best positioned to benefit from these trends, as new projects are hampered by long permitting timelines.

## Sector Focus

For 2026, Rick Rule's primary focus is on several key natural resource sectors. He identifies oil & gas as the 'best sector' for the next five years. He is also bullish on uranium, citing a classic bull market setup with rising term contracting and lagging supply. Copper is another key area due to multi-decade underinvestment. Lastly, he includes precious metals, with gold serving as a response to uncertainty and portfolio insurance, and silver offering higher torque and long-term potential due to its dual monetary and industrial nature, albeit with more volatility.

Rick Rule's recommended portfolio strategy for 2026 emphasizes a preference for established, cash-generating companies over speculative, high-risk junior explorers. He advocates for focusing on 'beta' (incumbents) rather than 'alpha' (speculative juniors). His approach involves owning well-run businesses, with a particular emphasis on mid-cap Canadian energy leaders known for their cash generation and disciplined capital allocation. Gold is used as a form of portfolio insurance. The overall strategy is to selectively own bellwethers in key sectors like uranium (e.g., Cameco) and quality incumbents in copper, while building a core around disciplined energy producers.

## Core Holdings Examples

The provided context mentions several specific examples of Rick Rule's core holdings, reflecting his focus on Canadian energy and top-tier producers. His named positions include: Canadian Natural Resources (CNQ), which he describes as a 'core holding' for its diversified exposure and disciplined management. In the uranium sector, Cameco is highlighted as a 'top pick'. He also owns positions in Canadian royalty companies and operators such as PrairieSky Royalty Ltd. (PSK), Freehold Royalties Ltd. (FRU), Tourmaline Oil Corp. (TOU), and Birchcliff Energy Ltd. (BIR).

## Sector Views

Rick Rule has specific views on portfolio allocation within natural resources. He believes oil & gas is the 'best sector' and the 'best 5-year bet' due to it being a hated, capital-starved industry with improving returns. He sees a 'classic bull market setup' in uranium, making leading producers like Cameco a key allocation. For copper, he favors incumbent producers who will benefit from the supply crisis caused by decades of underinvestment. Regarding precious metals, he views gold not as a speculative play but as essential 'insurance' that responds to uncertainty. He notes silver has long-term upside due to its industrial and monetary roles but warns of its volatility and advises selling into speculative rallies.


# John Hathaway Analysis

## Investment Thesis

John Hathaway's core investment thesis for 2026 is centered on a bullish case for gold, which he believes is entering its 'next chapter' supported by persistent macroeconomic catalysts such as government deficits and policy shifts. He posits that due to liquidity constraints in the physical metal market, even a modest reallocation of capital by institutional investors could lead to a significant price increase, potentially doubling the price of gold to approximately $4,000 per ounce within two to three years from late-2025 levels.

## Key Arguments

Hathaway's thesis is supported by several key arguments. Firstly, he points to major financial institutions like Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley recommending that investors replace bonds in their portfolios with a significant gold component, ranging from 10% to 20%. Secondly, he identifies mergers and acquisitions (M&A) as a primary driver of returns in the mining sector, advocating for a strategy of owning high-quality assets that larger companies will feel compelled to acquire. Lastly, he highlights the structural supply deficit in the silver market, which has persisted for six consecutive years, reinforcing his overall bullish stance on precious metals.

## Market Outlook

John Hathaway's market outlook is highly positive for precious metals, with a specific price projection for gold to reach approximately $4,000 per ounce in the next two to three years. He anticipates a significant rerating of mining equity multiples as generalist investors, who are currently under-allocated, begin to rotate into the sector. This rotation, combined with strong M&A activity, is expected to drive substantial returns for investors in quality mining assets.


# John Hathaway Stock Picks And Portfolio

## Portfolio Strategy

This approach is centered on anticipating M&A activity, suggesting an overweight position in high-quality gold producers and developers that possess tier-one or strategic assets. The goal is to hold companies that are likely to become coveted acquisition targets as larger mining firms seek to replenish their reserves.

## Allocation Recommendation

Hathaway highlights recommendations from large banks like Goldman Sachs (10%) and Morgan Stanley (20%) that suggest replacing the bond component of a diversified portfolio with a significant allocation to gold. This implies his support for a substantial portfolio weighting in gold, in the range of 10-20%, to act as a hedge against macroeconomic instability and provide upside potential.

## Focus Area

The primary focus of John Hathaway's investment approach is on precious metals mining equities, in line with Sprott's mandate. His strategy involves overweighting quality gold producers and developers. Additionally, he maintains a bullish view on silver and recommends exposure to it, citing the metal's six consecutive years of a market supply deficit.


# Tavi Costa Analysis

## Investment Thesis

Tavi Costa, founder of Azuria Capital, posits that the world is in the early stages of a new, multi-year commodity supercycle. He argues that after decades of neglect and underinvestment, the commodities sector, particularly mining, is poised for a significant upcycle.

## Key Arguments

Costa's thesis is supported by several key arguments: 
1. **Structural Demand:** A surge in demand is driven by the buildout of AI and data centers, onshoring of supply chains, the transition to electric vehicles (EVs), and massive grid and infrastructure upgrades.
2. **Institutional Rotation:** Institutional capital is just beginning to rotate into the resource sector, which has been largely ignored for years. Costa notes that institutions are now "catching up with the idea of owning metals, owning resource companies."
3. **Macroeconomic Tailwinds:** A weakening U.S. dollar and rising fiscal deficits are creating favorable conditions for commodities.
4. **Decades of Underinvestment:** The sector has suffered from a prolonged period of capital starvation, leading to supply constraints that will be difficult to resolve quickly.

## Firm Strategy

Tavi Costa's new firm, Azuria Capital, focuses on identifying and executing long-term, asymmetric investment opportunities. The firm's strategy is based on deep macroeconomic and geological analysis. It has a particular focus on mining (both precious and critical metals), energy, and select emerging markets, with noted expertise in Latin American deals, such as the San Cristobal background.

Experts like Rick Rule emphasize its role in protecting against uncertainty and currency devaluation, while John Hathaway and Tavi Costa see it entering a new chapter or multi-year bull phase. The consensus is that gold is a core holding for navigating the financial landscape of 2026 and beyond.

Its monetary aspect allows it to rally alongside gold in response to currency debasement and uncertainty.

# Uranium Outlook

## Summary Outlook

The outlook for uranium is exceptionally bullish, with veteran resource investor Rick Rule describing it as a 'perfect setup' and a 'classic bull market'. The thesis is built on a foundational and structural supply deficit that is expected to persist, creating a favorable environment for producers. He recommends focusing on established companies like Cameco to capitalize on this trend.

## Supply Demand Dynamics

The market is characterized by a severe imbalance. Demand is surging as nations turn to nuclear power as part of the global energy transition. Conversely, supply is lagging significantly due to what Rick Rule terms a '15-20 year' delay in the necessary capital expenditure cycle for new mine development. This structural scarcity is the primary driver of the bull market.

## Market Structure

A key feature of the uranium market is the role of long-term supply contracts. As utilities move to secure future supply, there is a trend of rising term contracting. This market mechanism is crucial as it provides predictable, long-term cash flow for established producers, de-risking their operations and investment appeal. Rick Rule highlights Cameco as a prime example of a producer benefiting from this structure and names it his 'top pick'.


# Copper Outlook

## Summary Outlook

The outlook for copper is highly bullish, based on a severe and long-term supply-demand imbalance. Rick Rule refers to the situation as a 'copper crisis' stemming from decades of underinvestment, while Tavi Costa sees an 'explosive' opportunity set. The consensus is that structural scarcity will drive prices higher, benefiting established producers.

## Investment Thesis

The core investment thesis, articulated by Rick Rule, centers on a massive and prolonged period of underinvestment in new copper supply, spanning approximately 30 years. This has created a structural deficit that cannot be quickly resolved. This scarcity is now colliding with a wave of demand from the energy transition and technological buildouts (AI, data centers, EVs, grid upgrades), creating what Tavi Costa describes as an 'explosive' setup.

## Key Challenge

The primary challenge, which also forms part of the bullish thesis for incumbents, is the immense difficulty and long timelines associated with bringing new copper mines into production. Rick Rule notes that the capex cycle started '15–20 years too late'. This tension between ambitious policy goals for electrification and the physical reality of mine development, including permitting hurdles, means that supply will struggle to meet demand for the foreseeable future, benefiting existing producers.

# Comparative Analysis

## Areas Of Agreement

All three experts—Rick Rule, John Hathaway, and Tavi Costa—show strong agreement on the foundational reasons for being bullish on natural resources and hard assets through 2026. They concur that a long period of underinvestment has created structural supply deficits across key commodities. They all point to a deteriorating macroeconomic backdrop, characterized by high government deficits and declining confidence in fiat currencies, as a primary catalyst that will drive capital into tangible assets. Furthermore, all three view gold as a crucial portfolio component, whether for its role as 'insurance' against uncertainty (Rule), as a replacement for bonds amid institutional rotation (Hathaway), or as part of a broad commodity upcycle (Costa). They also share a bullish view on silver, with Hathaway noting its six consecutive years of supply deficit.

## Differences In Strategy

While the experts agree on the macro thesis, their primary focus and recommended strategies show key differences. 

*   **Rick Rule's Focus on 'Beta' and Cash Flow:** Rule's strategy prioritizes established, cash-generating producers, which he refers to as 'beta.' He favors incumbent companies in oil & gas, uranium, and copper that are well-positioned to benefit from rising commodity prices. His specific recommendations are concentrated in mid-cap Canadian energy companies known for capital discipline and shareholder returns (e.g., Canadian Natural Resources).

*   **John Hathaway's Focus on M&A:** Hathaway's strategy is centered on capturing returns through mergers and acquisitions in the gold mining sector. His approach is to identify and own 'quality assets that other companies feel they must own.' This implies a focus on high-quality producers and developers with strategic deposits that are likely targets for consolidation, anticipating a rerating as generalist investors return and M&A activity accelerates.

*   **Tavi Costa's Focus on Asymmetric 'Alpha':** Costa's strategy is to find long-term, asymmetric investment opportunities, often in earlier-stage companies. He seeks 'discovery-driven alpha,' as exemplified by his activist precious metals portfolio at Crescat Capital, which focused heavily on exploration-stage companies like Tectonic Metals. His new venture, Azuria Capital, continues this search for asymmetric setups in mining and energy, with a particular expertise in Latin America.


# Macroeconomic Backdrop

The bullish commodity theses from all three experts for 2026 are underpinned by a shared view of a volatile and shifting macroeconomic landscape. A primary driver is the declining confidence in fiat currencies, particularly the U.S. dollar. Rick Rule explicitly forecasts a potential 75% drop in the dollar's purchasing power over the next decade, while Tavi Costa sees a weakening dollar as a key tailwind for commodities. This is driven by persistent and rising government deficits. Central bank and institutional policies are also seen as a major catalyst; John Hathaway highlights that major banks like Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley are recommending large portfolio allocations (10-20%) to gold, signaling a significant shift away from traditional assets like bonds. Furthermore, the general environment of uncertainty and geopolitical risk makes hard assets like gold attractive as 'portfolio insurance.' Finally, new structural demand drivers, including the massive energy requirements for the AI and data center buildout, the reshoring of industrial supply chains, and the ongoing energy transition, are creating a powerful, long-term demand pull for energy and metals.

# Identified Investment Risks

The research highlights several potential risks and cautionary notes that could impact the bullish investment theses for natural resources. These include:

*   **Commodity Price Volatility:** Natural resource markets are inherently volatile, and prices can fluctuate significantly, affecting investment outcomes and timelines. Rick Rule notes that while he is long-term bullish on silver, investors should be prepared to sell speculative gains due to its volatility.
*   **Policy and Permitting Risk:** Government intervention, particularly in the form of policy changes and lengthy or difficult permitting processes, poses a significant risk. This is noted as a particular challenge for the copper sector, where bringing new supply online is notoriously difficult.
*   **Financing Conditions for Juniors:** The financial health and prospects of smaller, exploration-stage companies (juniors) are highly dependent on capital markets. Changes in financing conditions can impact their ability to fund operations and advance projects.
*   **Macroeconomic Shifts:** Unforeseen changes in macroeconomic variables, such as interest rates and foreign exchange (FX) rates, can alter the investment landscape and affect the performance of commodity-related assets.
*   **General Caveat:** The analysis and opinions from the experts do not constitute personalized investment advice. Investors are reminded that position sizing and implementing proper risk controls are essential when investing in this sector.
