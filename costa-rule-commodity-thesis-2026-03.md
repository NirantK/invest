# Tavi Costa Investment Thesis

Tavi Costa's macro investment thesis, as of March 2026, is centered on the belief that a new commodity supercycle is in its early stages. Through his firm, Azuria Capital, he focuses on long-term asymmetric strategies in mining, energy, and Latin America. His core argument is that a combination of structural demand drivers—including AI data centers, industrial onshoring, EVs, and infrastructure spending—is colliding with a decade of underinvestment in commodity supply. He points to historically low capital expenditures, a lack of large new discoveries, and stagnant production as evidence of a constrained supply side. Costa believes the entire resource industry is undervalued, representing only about 1% of global equity markets, and that institutional rotation into the space is just beginning.

In precious metals, he turned more constructive in March 2026 following what he termed one of the 'most extreme' selloffs in silver history. He argues it is 'time to step in,' favoring mining equities—including seniors, juniors, and explorers—over physical metal for their potential leverage. He observed that during recent rebounds, 'silver and copper are leading, with juniors outperforming seniors and explorers showing strong upside.' Beyond precious metals, Costa highlights 'extremely cheap asymmetric trades' in other sectors, specifically mentioning energy, copper, and agriculture as areas where he is looking to deploy capital. While he discusses themes and how he would invest a sum like $10,000, his publicly accessible content in March 2026 did not name specific stock tickers, with some company-specific details being behind a paywall on his Substack.

# Rick Rule Investment Thesis

Rick Rule's investment philosophy in early 2026 is rooted in a value-oriented, contrarian approach, with a strong focus on the undervaluation of resource equities. He makes a clear distinction between gold and silver in a portfolio. He considers physical gold a core 'savings asset' to be held defensively for the long term. In contrast, he views silver as more speculative. This was demonstrated by his recent major portfolio shift where he sold approximately 80% of his physical silver holdings after its price surged to ~$75/oz, well past his original $50/oz target. He stated that the 'easy silver trade is over' and that the 'real money is in stocks.' He redeployed the proceeds from his silver sale into physical gold, select silver equities, and oil and gas stocks.

Rule is particularly bullish on mining equities, arguing there is a significant valuation disconnect. He notes that miners' earnings are often modeled using a gold price around $3,300/oz, and if gold's price realizes much higher levels (e.g., $5,200/oz), these companies are positioned for 'a positive earnings surprise.' His bullishness extends to other sectors:
- **Oil & Gas:** He favors oil and gas equities due to a backdrop of chronic underinvestment in sustaining capital, which, when combined with geopolitical supply risks from a war, will lead to tighter supplies and higher prices.
- **Uranium:** He maintains a constructive multi-year view, believing an improving term contract market will lower the cost of capital for developers, directly benefiting companies like NextGen Energy (NXE).

His specific, publicly named top picks from early 2026 include B2Gold (BTG), Amerigo Resources (ARREF), and the oilfield services giant SLB (SLB).

# Gold And Silver Outlook

Rick Rule and Tavi Costa present differing near-term strategies for precious metals as of March 2026, though both are fundamentally constructive. Rick Rule has taken a more cautious and tactical approach, stating he sold approximately 80% of his physical silver holdings after its price surged to around $75/oz, overshooting his original $50/oz target. He described this as getting 'out of the way of speculation' and has redeployed the proceeds into physical gold, select silver equities, and oil and gas stocks. Rule now believes 'the easy silver trade is over' and that 'the real money is in stocks.' He continues to hold physical gold as a core 'savings asset' for defensive purposes and wealth preservation. He is also constructive on gold miners, such as his top pick B2Gold (BTG), viewing them as undervalued, particularly if their earnings are repriced to reflect gold prices significantly higher than the $3,300/oz currently used in many models. In contrast, Tavi Costa views the recent pullback in precious metals as a significant buying opportunity. In a March 19 Substack post titled 'Gold: Time to Step In,' he argued that the selloff was extreme and that the sector presents an asymmetric trade. His bullish thesis is built on fundamental constraints: historically constrained supply, a capex cycle near record lows, a lack of large new discoveries, and stagnant production. He notes the entire industry represents only about 1% of global equity markets, suggesting significant room for institutional rotation. Costa favors miners, including seniors, juniors, and explorers, and has observed that juniors tend to outperform seniors during rebounds.

# Uranium Sector Outlook

Rick Rule presented a bullish, multi-year thesis for the uranium sector in March 2026. He believes that uranium equities are currently 'very cheap' when compared to the trajectory he anticipates for the term contract market over the next five years. A key driver of his thesis is the improving financing environment for uranium companies. He notes that as utilities sign long-term contracts with credit-grade counterparties, it will significantly lower the cost of capital for the entire uranium sector, making it easier and cheaper for companies to fund development and construction. Rule specifically highlighted developers as major beneficiaries of this trend, mentioning Lee Currier at NextGen Energy (NXE) as an example of a company well-positioned to take advantage of these dynamics. While he did not provide a specific allocation in the cited interview, in a separate discussion he has suggested that a 5-10% portfolio allocation to uranium can be appropriate.
